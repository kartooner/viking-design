Viking is a design methodology for AI-native products. It gives your team a clear sequence from value assessment to engineering handoff, so you build the right thing before you build the thing.

The problem

Most design frameworks were built before AI existed.

They assume your product does what you tell it to do. AI products don't work that way. They fail in unexpected ways, require human judgment at odd moments, and need a different kind of design thinking to get right.

01
No starting point

Teams jump straight to screens before asking whether AI is even the right tool for the problem.

02
No system thinking

Designers work on surfaces without mapping the logic underneath. The AI behavior surprises everyone.

03
No handoff language

Engineers get wireframes instead of contracts. What the AI should do, and when, gets lost in translation.

The methodology

Five steps. In order. Every time.

Viking is a sequence, not a toolkit. Each step builds on the last. You don't skip to screens until the thinking is done.

01
The 5 Gates

Before anything else, assess whether AI is the right tool. Five questions covering Value, Precision, Logic, Risk, and Context that save you from building the wrong thing entirely.

Fit assessment
02
Things, States, Rules

Map your product's object model before touching the canvas. What are the things? What states can they be in? What rules govern them?

Logic mapping
03
The High Council

A seven-role skill chain that walks you from strategy to engineering handoff. Each role has a clear mandate and hands off to the next.

Design process
04
Generative UI Recipes

Think procedural generation in video games. You define the rules and the system assembles the right UI for the moment. No fixed screens. Just surfaces that fit the context they appear in.

Interface design
05
The AI Slop Test

A quality audit to catch generic, AI-generated aesthetics before they ship. Clean typography and real design judgment. Not pattern soup.

Quality audit
